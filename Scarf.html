I don’t smoke, except when I do

<<<<<<<< Updated upstream:Scarf.html A scarf for Eva. Inspired by characteristics like her taste in fashion or favourite drink.

======== I don’t consider myself a smoker, but I know how to enjoy the effects of it, both physical and social. I therefore sometimes join my peers in the sacred ritual of the cigarette. It has happened many times that someone expressed surprise towards it. “You smoke?” I respond with an ironic slogan drenched in a James Bond-like perversion of coolness: “I don’t smoke, except when I do.” I like how it lets me engage in the status and smugness of the non-smoker, while shattering that through the hypocrisy and reminder that like everybody else, I am a child of God, a sinner, an ensemble of flesh and blood, engaging in self-destructive behaviour against my better judgement.
